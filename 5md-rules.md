**5-Minute Dungeon** is a fast-paced, cooperative card game where players work together to defeat a series of dungeons, each within a strict five-minute time limit. There are **no turns**; everyone plays simultaneously, racing against the clock to match symbols on dungeon cards.

### **1. Setting Up the Game**
*   **Hero Selection:** Each player chooses one of twelve heroes and places their corresponding Hero Mat face up. 
*   **Hero Decks:** Players take the deck matching the color of their Hero Mat, shuffle it, and place it on their "Draw Pile" space.
    *   *Note for Two-Player Games:* Each player selects a second deck and shuffles both together to have enough cards to defeat the dungeon.
*   **Starting Hand Size:** Draw cards based on the number of players:
    *   **2 players:** 5 cards each.
    *   **3 players:** 4 cards each.
    *   **4-6 players:** 3 cards each.
*   **Building the Dungeon Deck:** Place a Boss Mat in the center (starting with Boss #1, the Baby Barbarian). Count out the number of Door Cards indicated on the Boss Mat, add **2 Challenge Cards per player**, shuffle them, and place them on the Boss Mat.

### **2. Gameplay Mechanics**
The game begins when the timer starts and the first dungeon card is flipped.

#### **Defeating Dungeon Cards**
Most cards have symbols (Swords, Arrows, Scrolls, Jumps, or Shields) that must be matched to defeat them. There are three ways to do this:
1.  **Resource Cards:** Players "slap down" cards to the center of the table until all symbols on the dungeon card are matched.
2.  **Action Cards:** Specific cards like "Fireball" can instantly defeat certain types of threats (e.g., Monsters).
3.  **Special Abilities:** Each hero has a unique ability on their mat. To use it, a player must **discard 3 cards** to their personal discard pile and announce the ability.
    *   If an ability defeats a specific type (Monster, Obstacle, Person), it can only be used when facing that card type.
    *   Other abilities can be used at any time.

#### **Rules for Playing Cards**
*   **A card laid is a card played:** Once a card is in the center, it cannot be taken back.
*   **Refilling:** Every time you play or discard a card, immediately refill your hand to your starting size.
*   **Discarding:** Only discard to use an ability or if an Event Card specifically instructs you to. These cards go to your personal discard pile.
*   **Strategic Discards:** You can play non-matching Resource Cards to the center to "cycle" through your deck, but these cannot be recovered by healing abilities.

### **3. Dungeon Elements**
*   **Door Cards:** These are the primary obstacles (Monsters, Obstacles, or People).
*   **Event Cards:** Identified by a star icon, these require immediate action (e.g., "All players discard a card"). Some Action Cards, like "Cancel," can stop these effects.
*   **Mini-Bosses:** These are extra-tough Challenge Cards that require many symbols and **cannot** be defeated by standard Action Cards (like Fireball) or hero abilities.
*   **Dungeon Boss:** The final challenge on the Boss Mat. When facing a Boss, you **cannot** play non-matching Resource Cards or Action Cards that target Monsters/People/Obstacles (as Bosses are a separate category).

### **4. Winning and Losing**
*   **Winning:** You win a dungeon by defeating all cards in the deck and then matching the symbols on the Boss Mat before time runs out. To win the entire game, you must conquer all seven dungeons, ending with The Dungeon Master Final Form.
*   **Losing:** The team loses if:
    1.  The 5-minute timer runs out before the Boss is defeated.
    2.  All players run out of cards and have no way to draw more.
    3.  The team gets stuck on a card with no remaining way to defeat it.

If you lose, reset the timer, rebuild the dungeon deck, and try again. If you win, sort the cards back into their decks and prepare the next dungeon.

# Notes:
If two players play the last needed symbol at the same time, **both cards are lost** and cannot be recovered. 

The sources state a fundamental rule for the game: "**A card laid is a card played**". This means that once any card is placed in the center of the table, it cannot be taken back. Because the game is played simultaneously without turns, it is common for players to accidentally "over-match" a card in their haste. 

Here is what happens to those cards according to the rules:

*   **No Take-Backs:** Once those cards hit the table, they are considered played, even if one of them was technically redundant.
*   **Swept Away:** When a dungeon card is defeated, it is swept away along with **all cards played to defeat it**. Both symbols would be included in this pile.
*   **Hand Refill:** Despite losing the card, the player is not at a long-term disadvantage because you must **refill your hand** to its starting size immediately after playing or discarding any card.

**The Only Exception**
The only time you are required to take a card back into your hand is when **facing a Boss**. In a Boss fight, you are not allowed to play non-matching resource cards or certain action cards; if you play an invalid card in this specific scenario, you must return it to your hand. However, because the symbol you played was a match for what was needed, it remains a valid play and would still be "lost" to the center pile.
